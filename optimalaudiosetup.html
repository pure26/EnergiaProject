Laptop or Surface Pro Users

If you are using a laptop's built-in microphone and speakers while connected to a docking station, make sure your laptop's lid is open. Otherwise, meeting participants may not be able to hear you. The microphone is usually located next to your camera.

Also, please make sure that the docking station is connected properly.

To use your laptop’s microphone and speaker kindly follow the steps below: -

1. In the Skype for Business main window, go to Tools > Options > Audio Device,

2. In the Audio Device panel, select your preferred audio device.

Setting up your External Audio Device

1. In the Skype for Business main window, go to Tools > Options > Audio Device, and then choose the device you want.

2. In the Audio Device panel, select your preferred audio device.

3. Click the green arrow next to Speaker to hear a sample tone, and drag the slider if you need to adjust the volume.

4. To test your mic volume, speak for a few seconds. The volume is optimal when the progress bar reaches the middle of the test area. Drag the slider next to Microphone to adjust the mic volume.

5. Click Check Call Quality and follow the prompts to record a short message and play it back. Adjust audio as needed.

6. If you have more than one audio device, you can choose to set both to ring when you get a call. Under Secondary Ringer, select Also ring, and then select an additional device.

You can also select Unmute when my phone rings, which will automatically unmute your speakers when you receive a Skype for Business call.

7. Select Allow stereo audio playback when available to make sure that the best audio quality is available during a meeting.

8. Click OK when you’re done

If you do not see your device in the list, make sure it’s connected directly to your computer by searching Sound in Start Menu. Follow the instructions that came with the device to install it.

Tip

You can select different devices for microphone and speakers under each section. But to avoid echoing, it is better to use the same device for both. If you have to use separate devices, minimize echoing by lowering your speaker's volume.

Optimizing your Audio

Check Call Quality

Before you start a Skype for Business meeting or call, make sure your audio device sounds the way you want. Note that Check Call Quality may not be available for your account.

1. On the lower-left side of the main Skype for Business window, click Select Primary Device.

2. Click Check Call Quality and follow the prompts to record a short message and play it back.

3. If you’re not happy with the audio quality, go to Audio Device Settings to adjust your mic and speakers, or select another device.

Use a Headset

A headset provides two benefits: clarity and privacy. A headset microphone will pick up more of your voice and less of your surroundings than your computer's built-in microphone, and the earpiece(s).

Ensure that you use a USB dedicated headset, you may order a headset from the section above here.

Tip

Users should be aware that the headset connected through USB port is recommended over the one connected through the Headphone and Microphone ports since the latter sometimes is not compatible with Skype for Business

If your device has more than one set of inputs/outputs (built in mic/speakers, headset, external mic, etc.), take a moment to confirm that you are using your intended microphone and speakers.

Avoid echoing

If you are in a room with other participants who are attending the same Skype for Business meeting, and you are not using headsets, make sure the meeting's audio is only turned on for one of you. This will help eliminate disruptive echo and feedback. Using a headset or lowering the volume of your speakers/microphone can help avoid echoing issues.

Check Mute Button

Skype for Business mutes your microphone when you first join a meeting, so you'll need to remember to unmute yourself when you want to speak. Muting your microphone cuts down on background noise, keyboard clacking, and other distractions. All Skype for Business clients have an on-screen mute control. Users also can press Windows Key + F4 to mute/unmute themselves. Meeting organizers and presenters have the ability to mute individual participants or the entire audience if necessary.
