Before you Request the Recording Feature

To be able to record any Skype meeting, you should be the meeting organizer, and have the approved recording request.

If you are not the meeting organizer and you want to record the meeting, both the meeting organizer and yourself need to have the approved recording request.

Request Skype Recording Feature

1. Go to MyAccess page: https://myaccess.aramco.com.sa/

➢ Press on ‘Request Authorizations’

2. Skype for Business Meetings Recording – User Guide

➢ Follow the screenshot below:

Application ‘Skype for Business’ ➔ Authorization Name ‘skype recording’ ➔ Check the box ➔ press on ‘Add Selected Authorization’.

3. Skype for Business Meetings Recording – User Guide

➢ Check the box ➔ Add a Justification ➔ Press on ‘Submit’.

Re-login in to Skype Once Recording is Enabled.

Upon acquiring the recording feature, user must wait at least for 10 mins then sign out from Skype and sign in again for the recording feature to appear as an option during meetings.

4. Skype for Business Meetings Recording – User Guide

Start Recording

While in a meeting, follow the steps below to start and mange recording. Please note that the recorder must notify meeting’s participants that the conference call will be recorded.

1. Sharing recording publicly

Should the requester choose to publicly release the Skype recording, the requester must obtain the appropriate approvals (including from all participants) and comply with Saudi Aramco corporate GI 850.011 “Review and Approval of Saudi Aramco Digital Channels for an External Audience and Social Media Use”, GI 850.012 “Employees Interactions with External Media”, and GI 850.006 “Review and Approval of Information for Public Release”, Corporate Policy 15 (Records Management) and Corporate Policy 16 (Privacy and Data Protection). Only videos that are classified “Public Information” may be considered for public release.

2. Click the More Options button, and choose Start Recording.

3. Control recording

Use the controls at the bottom of the meeting to pause, resume, or stop the recording.

5. Skype for Business Meetings Recording – User Guide

4. Who else is recording?

To see who else is recording, point to the red recording button.

5. Stop recording

When you’re done, click Stop Recording.

A message will appear indicating that all recordings have stopped.

6. Manage your Skype recordings

Once recording has stopped, you can find and manage your recordings from Skype for Business Recording Manager.

6. Skype for Business Meetings Recording – User Guide

Wait for the recording to be processed and marked as Completed. Skype for Business automatically saves the recording in MP4 format that plays in Windows Media Player. You can exit the meeting while the recording is being processed.

7. Play recordings

When your recording has been processed, click your recording to highlight it, then click Play on the right side of the window to play the recording.

8. Rename and Delete recordings

You can also rename or delete your recording here.

7. Skype for Business Meetings Recording – User Guide

9. Publish options

You can save a second copy of the recording without one of or all of the following:

➢ Instant Messages

➢ Audio

➢ Presented content

➢ Participants videos

To do so, please follow the steps below:

➢ Before ending the meeting, click on the three dots at the right bottom corner of the page, then click of ‘Manage Recordings’.

➢ Wait for the recording to be processed and marked as Completed

8. Skype for Business Meetings Recording – User Guide

➢ Click on ‘Publish’

➢ Click on ‘Options’

Untick the options you want to the save the recording without, then clock on OK ➔ OK.

➢ Wait for the recording to be processed and marked as Completed. After that, the new copy will be saved to the specified location.

10. Retention period

You must not keep the recording for longer than is required for your purpose. This should never be stored for more than 2 years after which time it is your responsibility to delete the file. If you intend to retain for more than 2 years, the users should be made aware of this at the time the recording is to take place.

11. Browse and share recordings

To share your recording with internally others, click Browse to locate it, then copy the file to post on a shared location, such as OneDrive or an internal SharePoint site.
